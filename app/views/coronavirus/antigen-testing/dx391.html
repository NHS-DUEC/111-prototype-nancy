You and the people you live with should not leave your home

Your symptoms suggest that you could have a coronavirus infection.

Do not leave your home if you have symptoms or live with someone who does.

This is called self-isolation. Self-isolation is for people with symptoms and is different to social distancing, which is for everyone.

If you are self-isolating, you must:

  • not go out to buy food or collect medicine – order them by phone or online, or ask someone else to drop them off at your home
  • not have visitors, such as friends and family, in your home

You can use your garden, if you have one. Any exercise should be done at home.

How long to isolate for and managing symptoms at home

NHS.UK has lots of information to help if you have symptoms. You can find out:

  • how long to isolate for if you or someone you live with has symptoms
  • how to look after yourself and manage your symptoms at home

Get tested for coronavirus

You can apply to be tested to confirm a coronavirus infection.

We’ll ask you for some details. The COVID response service will call you and tell you what happens next.
