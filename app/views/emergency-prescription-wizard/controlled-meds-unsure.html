Controlled drugs

Some prescription medicines are called ‘controlled drugs’ because they can only be given to you by a doctor. You can’t get any of this medicine from a pharmacy without a doctor’s permission.

Controlled medicines are most often used to treat pain.

They’re medicines like:

  • morphine
  • pethidine
  • methadone
  • temazepam
  • diazepam
  • codeine

Some hormones and strong doses of steroids are controlled.

Most medicines are not controlled

Most tablets, creams and sprays for long term conditions, skin problems and infections are not controlled drugs.

For example, prescribed medicine for:

  • blood pressure
  • cholesterol
  • diabetes
  • asthma
  • infections
  • depression
  • heart conditions
  • skin conditions
