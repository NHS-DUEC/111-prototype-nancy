Controlled meds unsure

Prescription medicines come in 2 groups: controlled and non-controlled.

Most antibiotics and medicines to treat long term conditions are non-controlled.

For example, prescribed medicine for:

  • blood pressure
  • cholesterol
  • diabetes
  • asthma
  • infections
  • depression
  • heart conditions

These are more likely to be non-controlled medicine.

Controlled medicines are most often used to treat pain. They’re medicines like:

  • morphine
  • pethidine
  • methadone
  • temazepam
  • diazepam
  • codeine

You can’t get these from a pharmacy without seeing a doctor first.
