Get help as soon as you can

Before you get help

Show Hide

If you think you might report the assault to the police:

  • cover any cuts, but don’t wash them
  • don’t change or wash your clothes

Call 999 to report it to the police. GOV.UK has information on what happens when you report a rape or sexual assault.

Caring for any injuries:

Wounds
  • When the wound is checked, you may need to be given a tetanus immunisation.
  • If the wound is bleeding:
    • Use a dry dressing or a clean cloth to make a pad and press it firmly and directly onto the wound to stop the bleeding.
    • If the blood soaks through the pad, don't take it off. Place another pad on top of it and keep pressing it onto the wound.
    • If you're helping someone else, try to keep their blood off your skin.
    • Try to raise the limb above the level of your heart and continue to apply pressure. Don't use a tourniquet to stop the bleeding, unless one is already in place, in which case, do not remove it.
    • If the bleeding stops, secure the pad with a bandage or sticky tape. Make sure it's firm but not too tight.
    • Use pillows or a sling to keep the injured area raised.
  • If the wound is not bleeding:
    • Cover it with a plaster, a non-stick dressing or a non-fluffy clean cloth.
    • If you can, raise the injured area with pillows or a sling to above the level of the heart.
    • If it starts to bleed again fold a cloth into a pad and press firmly, directly onto the wound to stop the bleeding.
Medication, pain and/or fever
  • Paracetamol or ibuprofen can be used to relieve pain or a fever. Don't take if you've been told not to, or you've already taken some. Read the instructions on the packet, or talk to a pharmacist.
  • If you're pregnant: Use paracetamol. Don't use ibuprofen unless a doctor, midwife or pharmacist has advised you to.
Swelling, trauma
  • Don't have anything to eat or drink before going to the hospital. A sip of water is fine, if you need to take medicine.
  • Remove any tight clothing or jewellery from around the swelling.
  • Wrap an ice pack (or bag of frozen peas) in a cloth and put it on the swelling for 15-20 minutes. Repeat every 2-3 hours when you are awake.
  • Never put ice directly on your skin and don't leave ice on while you're asleep.
  • If your leg is affected:
    • Raise a swollen ankle or knee by sitting with your foot on a stool, or lying with it propped up on pillows.
  • If your arm or hand is affected:
    • Raise a swollen hand or wrist above the level of your heart using a sling. Use pillows in bed to raise it up.

The best place to get help is Whitechapel Haven

The Havens are specialist centres in London for people who have been raped or sexually assaulted.

Open 24 hours but you must phone 020 3299 6900 first for advice and an appointment.
